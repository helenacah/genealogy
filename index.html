Patrick Cahill

1894 – 1917 • Killed in Action at Vimy Ridge

Summary

Patrick Cahill was born in 1894 to Bridget Flanagan and Patrick Cahill. His sister Mary was born in 1895. When Patrick was just three years old, his father died. Bridget later remarried Denis Hayes and had nine more children.

Patrick emigrated to the United States in 1912 at the age of 17. In 1913 he travelled to Canada and enlisted in the Canadian Army in 1915.

On April 9th, 1917 (Easter Monday), Patrick took part in the Battle of Vimy Ridge. He was killed in action that day at the age of 23 and is buried in France.

Family Background

Patrick’s mother, Bridget Flanagan, was a sister of Michael Flanagan, making Patrick a first cousin of Ellen Flanagan.

Photo Gallery

Military Service

Patrick enlisted in Edmonton, Alberta, on May 14th, 1915. His regimental number was 437244.

On April 18th, 1916, Patrick sailed aboard the SS Missambie to England and later proceeded to France.

Burial

Patrick is buried at the Cabaret-Rouge British Cemetery in Nord-Pas-de-Calais, France.

Official record: Canadian Virtual War Memorial

Battle of Vimy Ridge

The Battle of Vimy Ridge took place between April 9th and April 12th, 1917, as part of the Battle of Arras.

Over 10,000 Canadian casualties were suffered, including 3,500 killed.
